# Project Chimera Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Our standards for how we interact and treat each other are grounded in our
core values:

### Respect

We value each other's time, effort, and contributions. We treat everyone with
respect and professionalism, regardless of their experience level or background.

### Inclusivity

We welcome and encourage participation from everyone. We actively work to create
an environment where diverse perspectives are valued and everyone can contribute.

### Collaboration

We believe in the power of collaboration. We share knowledge, support each other's
growth, and work together to build something greater than any individual could alone.

### Transparency

We communicate openly and honestly. We make decisions in the open and share our
reasoning with the community.

### Learning

We embrace mistakes as learning opportunities. We ask questions, seek help when
needed, and support others in their learning journey.

## Expected Behavior

### In All Spaces

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accept constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members

### In Communication

- Listen before responding
- Assume good intentions
- Ask for clarification when needed
- Keep discussions focused and constructive
- Respect different communication styles and time zones

### In Code and Contributions

- Write clear, documented code
- Follow the project's coding standards
- Provide helpful code review feedback
- Be patient with newcomers
- Give credit where it's due

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in our community:

- Harassment, discrimination, or derogatory language
- Personal attacks or insulting comments
- Trolling or deliberately provoking others
- Publishing private information without permission
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Any conduct that could be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Accessibility and Inclusion

Project Chimera is committed to accessibility. We expect all contributors to:

- Follow WCAG guidelines for web content
- Provide alternative text for images
- Use clear, simple language in documentation
- Consider accessibility features in design decisions
- Be receptive to accessibility feedback

## Reporting Issues

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns,
please report it by:

**Email:** conduct@project-chimera.org

**Private Message:** Message any of the project maintainers directly

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information (so we can follow up)
- The nature of the incident
- The individuals involved (if known)
- Any relevant context or evidence
- Any suggested actions

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible. We will only share
information with those who need to know to address the situation.

### Response Timeline

We will acknowledge your report within 24 hours and provide a response within
7 days with the next steps.

## Enforcement

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

### Consequences

Project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning
the offender or temporarily or permanently banning them from the community.

### Appeals

If you feel you have been unfairly accused or sanctioned, you may appeal to
the project leadership by emailing conduct@project-chimera.org.

## For Students

Project Chimera is a student project, and we recognize that students are learning
not just technical skills, but also how to collaborate in professional environments.

### Learning Environment

We view mistakes as learning opportunities. Our response to student conduct issues
will prioritize education and growth over punishment.

### Mentorship

Senior students and project leaders are expected to mentor junior students,
providing guidance not just on technical matters, but also on professional
conduct and collaboration.

### Academic Integrity

All contributors must maintain academic integrity. Code should be your own work
or properly attributed. When using external resources, cite your sources.
