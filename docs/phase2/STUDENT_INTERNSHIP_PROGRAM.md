# Project Chimera Phase 2 - Student Internship Program

**Program Type**: Paid Internship
**Duration**: 3 months (June - August 2026 or September - November 2026)
**Stipend**: £400-500/month depending on role
**Hours**: 5-10 hours per week
**Positions Available**: 5

---

## Program Overview

### Purpose
The Project Chimera Student Internship Program provides paid learning opportunities for students to contribute to an innovative AI-powered live theatre project while gaining real-world software development experience.

### Phase 1 Lessons Learned

**What Didn't Work**:
- Volunteer model resulted in 0 student participation (99.8% single author)
- Complex onboarding (Kubernetes, Docker) was barrier to entry
- 8-week timeline was insufficient for student onboarding
- No accountability or incentives for completion

**Phase 2 Improvements**:
- **Paid stipends** provide financial incentive
- **Simplified onboarding** focuses on Git basics only
- **3-month duration** allows meaningful contribution
- **Mentorship pairs** provide guidance and support
- **Clear deliverables** with defined success criteria

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to be a BCU student?
**No**: Students from any university can apply. Recent graduates are also welcome.

### Do I need to be a computer science student?
**No**: While CS students are well-suited, we welcome students from any background with relevant skills or interest. The BSL Researcher position, for example, may suit linguistics or accessibility students.

### Can I work more than the specified hours?
**No**: The internship is designed for part-time work around studies. Additional hours are not compensated.

### Can this lead to a full-time job?
**Possibly**: While we can't guarantee employment, successful interns will receive recommendations and may be considered for future Phase 3 or other projects.

### What if I need to take time off during the internship?
**Accommodated**: We understand that exams, holidays, or personal commitments may require time off. Communicate with your mentor in advance.

### Do I need my own computer?
**Yes**: You need access to a computer and internet. Most development work can be done on a standard laptop.

### Will I be paid if I don't complete the internship?
**Pro-rated**: If you leave mid-internship for valid reasons, you'll be paid for completed work. Abandoning the internship without notice may result in forfeited payment.
